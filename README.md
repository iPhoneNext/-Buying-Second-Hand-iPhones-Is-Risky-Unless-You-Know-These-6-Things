# 1. The iCloud Lock Problem Most Buyers Don’t Know About

One of the most common — and expensive — mistakes people make when buying **used iPhones in South Africa** is not checking whether the device is still linked to the previous owner’s Apple ID.

This is called an **iCloud lock**.

If an iPhone is still connected to someone else’s Apple account, it cannot be properly set up. Even after a factory reset, the device will ask for the previous owner’s login details.

Without those credentials, the phone becomes essentially unusable.

That’s why experienced buyers always insist on seeing the phone **fully reset and ready for activation** before purchasing.

A legitimate seller should always guarantee that their devices are unlocked and ready to use.

---

# 2. Battery Health Matters More Than People Realise

Another issue many buyers overlook is battery performance.

When browsing listings for **cheap used iPhones**, it’s easy to focus on price and storage capacity while ignoring battery health completely.

But battery condition plays a huge role in how a phone performs daily.

A device with extremely low battery health may require multiple charges throughout the day. For someone relying on their phone for work, communication, or travel, that quickly becomes frustrating.

Before buying any **refurbished iPhone**, buyers should check battery health in the settings menu and ask whether the battery has been tested or replaced.

Responsible sellers always verify battery performance before putting a device on sale.

# 3. When a Price Looks Too Good to Be True

If you’ve searched online for **second hand iPhones for sale**, you’ve probably seen listings that seem unbelievably cheap.

That’s usually a warning sign.

Extremely low prices often indicate hidden problems such as stolen devices, internal hardware damage, counterfeit parts, or activation locks.

While saving money is important, choosing the **cheapest option** is rarely the smartest choice.

A reliable seller focuses on value, transparency, and support — not unrealistic pricing.

---

# 4. Physical Condition Isn’t Just About Scratches

When people inspect a used phone, they often focus only on cosmetic appearance.

Small scratches or minor wear are normal for **previously owned iPhones**.

What matters more is functionality.

The camera, speakers, Face ID, buttons, charging port, and screen responsiveness all need to be tested properly. Internal components should also be verified to ensure the device hasn’t suffered water damage or hidden faults.

Professional sellers run complete quality checks before making devices available to customers.

Without that inspection process, buyers are essentially gambling.

Many buyers assume warranties only apply to brand-new phones.

That assumption can become expensive.

Without a warranty, any issue that appears after purchase becomes the buyer’s responsibility. A single repair could wipe out the savings of buying pre-owned.

That’s why the best sellers of **pre owned iPhones South Africa** include warranty coverage with their devices.

A proper warranty ensures that if something goes wrong under normal use, the customer isn’t left alone to deal with the cost.

Perhaps the biggest factor when buying a **used iPhone near you** isn’t the model or storage size.

It’s the seller.

Reliable sellers provide clear contact details, physical locations, customer reviews, and after-sales service. If problems arise, customers know exactly where to go.

When it comes to technology purchases, trust is everything.
